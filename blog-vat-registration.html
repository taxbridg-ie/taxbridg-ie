Understanding VAT Registration in Ireland: Thresholds & Deadlines

18 December 2024 7 min read TaxBridge Team

Value Added Tax (VAT) is one of the most complex areas of Irish tax compliance. Knowing when you must register, which rates apply, and how to file your returns correctly is essential for every Irish business owner. This guide covers everything you need to know about VAT registration in Ireland.

Key Takeaways

  • VAT registration threshold: €80,000 for goods; €40,000 for services
  • You must register within 30 days of exceeding the threshold
  • Voluntary registration is possible — and often beneficial
  • Standard VAT rate is 23%; reduced rates of 13.5% and 9% apply to certain supplies
  • Most businesses file bi-monthly VAT3 returns via ROS

VAT Registration Thresholds

In Ireland, you are legally required to register for VAT if your turnover exceeds — or is likely to exceed — the following annual thresholds:

Type of SupplyAnnual Threshold
Supply of goods€80,000
Supply of services€40,000
Mixed goods and services€80,000 (goods threshold if majority is goods)
Distance sales (intra-EU)€10,000 (EU-wide OSS threshold)
Registration Trigger

You must register as soon as you reasonably expect your turnover to exceed the threshold in the next 12 months — not only after you have actually exceeded it. Failure to register on time results in penalties and backdated VAT liability.

Should You Register Voluntarily?

Even if your turnover is below the threshold, you may choose to register for VAT voluntarily. This can be beneficial if:

  • Your customers are VAT-registered businesses (they can reclaim the VAT you charge, so it does not affect them)
  • You have significant VAT-inclusive input costs (you can reclaim the VAT on purchases)
  • You want to appear more established to larger business clients

However, voluntary registration also means additional compliance obligations (filing returns, keeping records). It is not always the right choice — particularly for consumer-facing businesses whose customers cannot reclaim VAT.

How to Register for VAT

  1. Log in to ROS (Revenue Online Service) or myAccount
  2. Select "Manage Tax Registrations"
  3. Apply to register for VAT, providing your business details and estimated turnover
  4. Revenue will issue your VAT registration number, typically within 5–10 working days
  5. You can then begin charging VAT and must file regular returns

VAT Returns — Frequencies and Deadlines

Most businesses file VAT3 returns on a bi-monthly (every 2 months) basis, due by the 19th of the month following the period end:

VAT PeriodReturn & Payment Due
January–February19 March
March–April19 May
May–June19 July
July–August19 September
September–October19 November
November–December19 January (following year)
Late Filing Penalty

Interest accrues at 0.0219% per day on unpaid VAT. There are also fixed penalties for late filing. Revenue takes VAT compliance seriously — it is one of the most frequently audited areas for small businesses.

Reclaiming VAT on Business Purchases

As a VAT-registered business, you can reclaim the VAT you pay on business purchases (input VAT). Valid VAT invoices must be retained for 6 years. Common reclaimable inputs include:

  • Office supplies, equipment, and software
  • Professional services (accountancy, legal, consulting)
  • Business travel and accommodation
  • Marketing and advertising costs

Note: VAT on entertainment and personal motor vehicles (below 2,000cc for diesel, passenger vehicles) is generally not reclaimable.
