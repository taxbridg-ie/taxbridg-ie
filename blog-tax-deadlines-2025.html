Key Irish Tax Deadlines You Cannot Afford to Miss in 2025

20 November 2024 5 min read TaxBridge Team

Missing a Revenue deadline is rarely just a minor inconvenience. Late filings trigger surcharges, interest accrues on unpaid tax from the day it was due, and repeated failures can draw unwanted Revenue attention. This guide gives you a comprehensive calendar of every significant Irish tax deadline for 2025.

Key Takeaways

  • The most important date is 31 October — self-assessment filing and payment deadline
  • Employers must remit PAYE/PRSI by the 23rd of each month
  • VAT3 returns are due by the 19th of the month after each bi-monthly period
  • Company annual returns to the CRO must be filed on time to avoid strike-off
  • PAYE workers can review and update their tax position via myAccount year-round

Critical Deadline: 31 December 2025

After 31 December 2025, you can no longer claim any reliefs or refunds for the 2021 tax year. If you have not yet reviewed your 2021 tax position, do it now — many PAYE workers are owed hundreds of euro from unclaimed medical expenses, rent tax credit, or flat-rate expenses for that year.

PAYE Workers: No Deadline Pressure

If you are a PAYE employee without non-PAYE income, you are not subject to the 31 October self-assessment deadline. You can review and claim your tax credits at any time through Revenue's myAccount service. However, be mindful of the 4-year limit — you can only claim for 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024 throughout 2025.

What Happens if You Miss a Deadline?

The consequences vary by obligation, but generally:

  • Late Form 11: 5% surcharge on tax due (max €12,695) if up to 2 months late; 10% (max €63,485) after 2 months
  • Late VAT return: Interest at 0.0219% per day on unpaid VAT; fixed penalties may apply
  • Late employer PAYE: Interest and potential prosecution for repeated failures
  • Late CRO annual return: Company may be struck off the register; late filing penalties of €100 per day (max €1,200) apply
