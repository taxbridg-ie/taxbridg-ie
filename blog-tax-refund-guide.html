How to Claim Your Full Tax Refund for 2021–2024: A Step-by-Step Guide

15 January 2025 7 min read TaxBridge Team

Did you know that Revenue allows PAYE workers to claim a tax refund going back four full tax years? If you have never filed a Form 12 or reviewed your tax credits, you could be owed hundreds — or even thousands — of euro. This guide walks you through every step of the process.

Key Takeaways

  • You can claim refunds for 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024 right now
  • The average unclaimed refund for a PAYE worker is €500–€1,500
  • Common claims include medical expenses, remote working relief, and rent tax credit
  • Claims are made through Revenue’s myAccount portal or via a registered tax agent
  • TaxBridge handles the entire process — you just provide the documents

Why Are So Many Refunds Unclaimed?

Most PAYE employees assume Revenue automatically calculates their correct tax position. While Revenue does issue a Preliminary End of Year Statement, it cannot account for reliefs and credits you have not actively claimed. Medical receipts, tuition fees, mortgage interest relief, and rental payments all require you to submit proof.

The 4-year limit is also not widely known. Many workers only discover they can claim historically when they finally speak to a tax professional.

Important Date

The deadline to claim for 2021 is 31 December 2025. If you miss this window, that tax year is gone forever.

What Can PAYE Workers Claim?

The following reliefs and credits are the most commonly missed by PAYE employees:

  • Medical Expenses: 20% tax relief on qualifying medical costs not covered by insurance. Keep all GP, consultant, and dental receipts.
  • Rent Tax Credit: Up to €1,000 per person (€2,000 per couple) in 2024. Up to €1,500 per person in 2025.
  • Remote Working Relief: 30% of broadband, electricity, and heating costs attributable to working from home.
  • Tuition Fees: 20% relief on third-level fees above €3,000 per course per year.
  • Flat-Rate Expenses: Pre-agreed allowances for nurses, teachers, journalists, and many other professions.
  • Health Insurance: Tax credit for approved insurer premiums paid by employees (not via payroll).
  • Pension Contributions: Relief at your marginal rate for Additional Voluntary Contributions (AVCs).

Step-by-Step: How to Claim

Option A — Claim Yourself via myAccount

  1. Go to revenue.ie and log in to myAccount (or register if you have not already).
  2. Click “Review Your Tax” under PAYE Services.
  3. Select the tax year you wish to review (start with the oldest: 2021).
  4. Add any reliefs you are entitled to — medical, rent, remote working, etc.
  5. Upload supporting receipts where prompted.
  6. Submit the claim. Revenue typically processes within 3–5 working days.
  7. Refunds are paid directly to your bank account.

Documents You Will Need

  • P60 or Employment Detail Summary for each year
  • Medical receipts (GP, hospital, dentist, physiotherapy)
  • Rent receipts or Residential Tenancies Board (RTB) confirmation
  • Remote working log (days worked from home per month)
  • Utility bills for home office calculation
  • Tuition fee receipts from your college or training provider

How Long Does It Take?

Revenue processes self-service claims within 3–5 working days in most cases. If submitted through a registered tax agent, the timeline is similar. Refunds are paid directly to your bank account as recorded in myAccount. For larger claims, Revenue may request additional documentation, which can add a few extra days.

Watch Out

Be accurate. Revenue can audit claims up to 4 years after filing. Always retain original receipts and only claim expenses you are genuinely entitled to.
