Top 5 Tax Reliefs Most PAYE Workers Miss Out On

28 January 2025 6 min read TaxBridge Team

Most PAYE employees assume they are paying the right amount of tax — and they often are. But what many do not realise is that there are numerous tax reliefs and credits available that Revenue will not apply automatically. You have to claim them. Here are the five most commonly overlooked reliefs and what they are worth.

Key Takeaways

  • Medical expenses give you 20% back on qualifying costs not covered by insurance
  • Remote working relief covers 30% of broadband, electricity, and heating costs
  • Flat-rate expenses are automatic for certain professions — but you still must claim
  • You can claim retrospectively for up to 4 years
  • The combined value of unclaimed reliefs can easily exceed €1,000 per year

Relief 1: Medical Expenses

Medical expenses are the most frequently unclaimed relief in Ireland. You are entitled to 20% tax relief on qualifying medical costs that were not reimbursed by private health insurance. This includes:

  • GP visits and prescription costs
  • Hospital consultant fees (not covered by insurance)
  • Physiotherapy and speech therapy
  • Dental treatment (excluding routine check-ups and cleanings)
  • Nursing home fees (60% relief)
  • In vitro fertilisation (IVF) costs

You can also claim for qualifying expenses paid on behalf of family members, even if they are not living with you. Keep every receipt — Revenue can request them during a review.

Example

If you spent €2,000 on GP visits, specialist consultations, and physiotherapy in a year, you would receive €400 back in your annual tax review.

Relief 2: Remote Working (e-Working) Relief

If you worked from home for any part of the year, you can claim relief on 30% of the broadband, electricity, and heating costs you incurred on your working days at home. You claim this based on a daily calculation: days worked from home ÷ total days in the year × 30% × annual utility bills.

Revenue accepts a simplified calculation — many employees claim approximately €200–€400 per year depending on their utility bills and the number of days worked from home.

Pro Tip

Keep a simple spreadsheet log of the days you worked from home each month. This is all Revenue needs to verify your claim — you do not need to submit your bills unless requested.

Relief 3: Flat-Rate Expenses

Many professions are entitled to a flat-rate expense allowance that covers the cost of uniforms, tools, and equipment. Revenue has agreed standard amounts for dozens of occupations, including:

  • Nurses / Midwives: €733 per year
  • Teachers (primary & secondary): €518 per year
  • Journalists / Reporters: €317 per year
  • Construction workers: €89–€179 depending on trade
  • Shop assistants / Retail: €121 per year
  • Doctors (in hospitals): €695 per year

The relief is worth 20–40% of the flat-rate amount depending on whether you are a standard or higher rate taxpayer. Once you apply it in one year, it should be carried forward automatically — but many workers have never applied it at all.

Relief 4: Tuition Fees (Third Level)

If you are paying tuition fees for yourself, a spouse, or a child at an approved third-level institution, you can claim 20% tax relief on fees above €3,000 per year, per course, up to a maximum of €7,000.

  • Applies to full-time and part-time undergraduate and postgraduate courses
  • Approved courses at IT and university level
  • Some professional development courses also qualify (IT, accountancy, law)
  • The college or provider must be on Revenue's approved list
Watch Out

The first €3,000 of fees per course are excluded. If your fees total less than €3,000 for the year, you do not qualify. The relief only kicks in on amounts above this threshold.

Relief 5: Pension Contributions (AVCs)

If you make Additional Voluntary Contributions (AVCs) to an approved pension scheme, you receive income tax relief at your marginal rate — either 20% or 40%. For a higher-rate taxpayer contributing €5,000 per year, this means an immediate tax saving of €2,000.

Age limits apply: the amount of income you can contribute to a pension at the marginal rate increases with age, up to 40% for those aged 60 or over. Many employees are significantly under their age-based limit and are missing out on years of tax-efficient pension saving.

Bonus: Health Insurance Credit

If you pay health insurance premiums from your own post-tax income (not via salary deduction through payroll), you may be entitled to a tax credit. In most employer schemes, this is applied automatically via tax relief at source (TRS), but self-payers should verify their position.
