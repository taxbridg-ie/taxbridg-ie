The Rent Tax Credit Explained: Up to €1,500 Back in 2025

10 February 2025 5 min read TaxBridge Team

The Rent Tax Credit was introduced in Budget 2023 and has been increased in subsequent budgets to help renters in Ireland offset high accommodation costs. For 2025, the credit is worth up to €1,500 per individual (€3,000 for a jointly assessed couple). Here is everything you need to know.

Key Takeaways

  • Worth up to €1,500 per person in 2025 (€3,000 per couple)
  • Available to tenants in private rented accommodation
  • You can also claim retrospectively for 2022, 2023, and 2024
  • The landlord must be registered with the RTB
  • Rent-a-room tenants and those in HAP do not qualify in most cases

Who Qualifies for the Rent Tax Credit?

To be eligible for the Rent Tax Credit in 2025, you must:

  • Pay rent for your principal private residence in Ireland
  • Have the tenancy registered with the Residential Tenancies Board (RTB)
  • Be a PAYE taxpayer or self-assessed individual paying income tax
  • Not be receiving a social housing payment such as HAP or RAS for the same property
  • Not be a student in full-time education (for 2022 claims; student accommodation is included from 2024)
2025 Update

From 2025, the credit has been extended to include student renters paying rent to an approved provider. If you are a student paying rent in private accommodation, you may now be eligible.

How Much Can You Claim?

The Rent Tax Credit amounts by year are:

Tax YearSingle PersonJointly Assessed Couple
2022€500€1,000
2023€750€1,500
2024€1,000€2,000
2025€1,500€3,000

The credit is a tax credit (not a deduction), meaning it reduces your tax bill euro-for-euro. However, you cannot receive more back than you have paid in tax. If your tax liability is lower than the credit amount, the excess is not refunded.

How to Claim the Rent Tax Credit

For PAYE Workers (via myAccount)

  1. Log in to revenue.ie/myAccount
  2. Select "Review Your Tax" for the relevant year
  3. Under "Tax Credits and Reliefs", find "Rent Tax Credit"
  4. Enter your landlord's details (name, PPSN if available, address)
  5. Enter the total rent paid in the year
  6. Submit — Revenue will apply the credit and issue a refund if applicable

For Self-Assessed Individuals

Claim via your Form 11 annual return under the appropriate credits section. TaxBridge can handle this for you as part of your annual return preparation.

Important

You must have your landlord's PPSN or property details to hand. Revenue cross-references this against RTB registration records. If your landlord is not RTB-registered, you cannot claim the credit.

Claiming Retrospectively for 2022–2024

If you have not yet claimed the Rent Tax Credit for 2022, 2023, or 2024, you can still do so. Simply log in to myAccount and complete a tax review for each year, adding the credit. Many renters have been leaving several thousand euros unclaimed by not doing this annual review.

Example

A single person who has rented since 2022 and never claimed could recoup: €500 + €750 + €1,000 + €1,500 = €3,750 in total credits across four years.
